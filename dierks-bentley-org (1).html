Who We Are

A Mission Rooted in
Faith, Music, & Service

The Dierks Bentley Organization was born from a simple belief: that music can move people, and that those who are moved have a responsibility to give back. Founded in 2008, we have grown from a grassroots effort into a nationally recognized nonprofit with programs serving veterans, underserved youth, and disaster-affected families across the United States.

Every initiative we run ties back to three core values — dignity, community, and access. We believe that where you were born should never determine what you are able to become, and that no one who has served this country should face hardship without a network of support.

Guided by our board of directors and a team of passionate staff and volunteers, we are committed to transparent stewardship of every dollar entrusted to us. We hold a 4-star rating from Charity Navigator and have maintained a program expense ratio above 85% every year since our founding.

🎖 Veterans First

Emergency housing, mental health access, and career placement services for veterans transitioning from active duty back into civilian life.

🎵 Music Education

Funding instruments, instruction, and after-school music programs in under-resourced schools, giving youth a creative voice of their own.

🌾 Rural Relief

Food security, mobile health clinics, and disaster response for rural communities that are often overlooked by mainstream aid networks.

🎓 Scholarship Fund

Merit and need-based college scholarships awarded annually to first-generation students from qualifying households across 12 states.

Our History

Fifteen Years of Giving Back

From a single benefit concert in Nashville to a multi-program national nonprofit, here is how the Dierks Bentley Organization has grown.

2008 — Founding Year

A Benefit Concert That Started It All

After a tour stop in Nashville brought Dierks Bentley face to face with families left homeless by a tornado in Hendersonville, Tennessee, he organized a benefit concert at the Ryman Auditorium. The event raised funds and sparked a desire to formalize the giving. The Dierks Bentley Organization was incorporated as a 501(c)(3) that same October.

$380,000 raised in Year 1

2010

Veterans Outreach Program Launches

Partnering with the Wounded Warrior Project and local VA chapters in Tennessee and Arizona, the organization launched its first structured Veterans Outreach Program, providing emergency housing vouchers and peer support to more than 600 veterans in the first year.

$1.2M directed to veterans services

2013

Music in Schools Initiative

After years of hearing from teachers about shrinking arts budgets, we launched Music in Schools — placing instruments, instructors, and after-school programs in 47 public schools across rural Kentucky, Tennessee, and West Virginia. By end of year, over 3,200 students had access to free music instruction for the first time.

$2.7M in grants awarded

2016

The Hurricane Relief Response

When Hurricane Matthew devastated coastal communities in Florida, Georgia, and the Carolinas, we mobilized within 48 hours. Working alongside local emergency management agencies, we deployed mobile kitchens, emergency supply kits, and short-term housing subsidies to over 4,100 displaced families.

$4.1M in emergency relief deployed

2018

A Decade of Giving — $20M Milestone

At our 10-year gala in Nashville, the DBO crossed the $20 million cumulative donation milestone. The event featured performances from more than 20 artists and drew philanthropic partners from across the country. That year, the organization expanded operations to 30 U.S. states.

$20M cumulative milestone reached

2020

COVID-19 Emergency Fund

In the first months of the pandemic, we redirected $3.8 million to an Emergency Stability Fund supporting furloughed music industry workers, food banks in underserved rural counties, and telehealth access for veterans who could not travel to VA facilities.

$3.8M emergency fund deployed

2022

Scholarship Endowment Established

A major gift of $5 million from an anonymous donor allowed the DBO to establish the Bentley Futures Scholarship Endowment — a permanent fund awarding renewable scholarships to first-generation college students in 12 states. The inaugural class of 80 scholars began their studies in the fall of 2022.

80 scholars, $5M endowment

2024

Expanding West — Pacific Programs

Building on our success in the South and Midwest, the DBO opened its first Western regional office in Phoenix, Arizona, extending our Veterans First and rural relief programs to underserved communities in Nevada, New Mexico, and California's Central Valley. We also launched a bilingual resource center serving Spanish-speaking families.

12,000 new beneficiaries in Year 1

What We Do

Active Programs & Initiatives

Each program is designed to address a specific, measurable need — with clear outcomes, community partners, and annual audits to ensure accountability.

🎖

Veterans First

Emergency housing, mental health referrals, and employment support for veterans transitioning to civilian life. Serving 9 states with 42 active partner organizations.

$11.4M raised to date · 23,000 veterans served

🎵

Music in Schools

Free instruments and professional instruction for students in underfunded rural public schools. Programs run in 140+ schools across 8 states.

$8.2M raised to date · 47,000 students reached

🌾

Rural Relief & Food Access

Mobile food pantries, emergency supply distribution, and disaster response coordinated with FEMA and county emergency services in rural counties.

$9.7M raised to date · 58,000 families helped

🎓

Bentley Futures Scholarship

Renewable college scholarships for first-generation students demonstrating financial need and community leadership. Up to $8,000 per year, per scholar.

$7.1M raised to date · 340 scholars funded

🏥

Mobile Health Clinics

Free preventive care, mental health screenings, and dental services deployed to rural towns that lack consistent access to healthcare professionals.

$3.8M raised to date · 31,000 patients served

🆘

Disaster Emergency Fund

A standing reserve activated within 48 hours of major declared disasters to provide immediate housing vouchers, meals, and supply kits to displaced families.

$5.9M raised to date · 19 disaster responses
